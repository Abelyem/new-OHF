Founder

Chief (Mrs) Comfort Titilola Adesoye Orija, Iya Laje of Oyo Empire. Born in Lagos on March 18th 1939 as the first child of the famous family of Late Mr. Edward Omolaja Orija (father) and Late Julianah Omolara Orija (mother) and first grandchild of Papa Samuel Bamigboye Orija. Her Father was from Abeokuta, whilst her mother (nee Adeyemi John) was from Oyo.

She was tutored by her aunt, a renowned teacher, the Late Mrs. Aina David, under whom she grew (along with personages including His Imperial Majesty Oba Lamidi Adeyemi, the Alaafin of Oyo, and Late Lanre John), who all lived with Late Sir Kofoworola Abayomi, her mother's uncle.

She attended Ireti Primary School, Breadfruit, Lagos as well as Yaba Methodist School and Abeokuta Girls' School. In 1959 she went to London to continue her education at Balham and Tooting College for the Distributive Trade to study Trade Art and Design.

She worked for John Lewis Partnership where she was the First Black African at that time to work with the company considering the high level then of racial discrimination in London. She returned to Nigeria in 1964 and joined A. G. Leventis, Marina, Lagos for a short spell before she proceeded to go into entrepreneurship wholesale (Hayes Head-ties).

In 1984, she returned to London to care for her seven children who to the glory of God today are respectively doing well. She continued her hospitality business by being the Pace-Setter in the marketing of African Take Away Dishes in London, which today is awash with African restaurants.

In 1990, she was inspired to form an NGO named 'OVER 50s WEST AFRICAN WOMEN FORUM" which she modified into a "62 WOMEN STRONG WEST AFRICAN WOMEN ASSOCIATION" (NAOWA) with six other women from Gambia, Namibia, Ghana and Nigeria. This strategic platform formed about 5 years ago is aimed at bringing about speedy socio-economic development to the global Yoruba Ancestral State of Oyo, Ogun, Ondo, Ekiti, Osun and Lagos Nigeria and beyond the African continent.

As the Iya-Laje of Oyo, Chief Mrs Comfort Titilayo Orija Adesoye, in 2015 founded Oranyan Heritage Foundation (OHF) as a major vehicle to drive the socio-cultural and economic responsibilities of her office. Traditionally, the office of Iya-Loja empowers her to lead the community through economic prowess. But Chief Mrs Orija-Adesoye, being from a rich cultural heritage is using the OHF in a broader format to touch nearly all aspects of develments, starting with the Museum of Black Women in History (MoBWIH).

Recently, she was appointed the Chairperson of her newly formed NGO named Oranyan Heritage Foundation (OHF) in partnership with Lumin-Artica, a consulting firm whom will oversee the structuring of the Museum of Black Women In History. OHF is a Non-Governmental Organisation, with the main objective of promoting and making the people of Oyo aware of the good work that His Imperial Majesty Oba Lamidi Adeyemi, Alaafin of Oyo, is doing in Yoruba land, for the Yoruba and to leave a good legacy for the younger ones. â€œMy vision is to build a Museum for Black Women all over the world, to celebrate their achievements from the past and present and that the legacy will carry on from generation to generation

God loves hard work and so she likes to work hard always. She is caring, but strongly believes in discipline. Her age notwithstanding, she loves appearing resplendently, she believes in the Yoruba proverbs which says "Sise sise ko gbodo ribi ole", "Mo ti d'agba mio s'oge mo, ko si owo lo nje be". ("A hardworking person cannot live like a lazy one", "I am too old for fashion is simply an expression of poverty"). She remains a very active member of the British Labour Party, she enjoys her private life, cherishes her business, and proud to be a business consultant. She is full of fulfilment and will continue to thank God.
